This Claude skill just replaced my entire SEO team. You've seen the posts. And for the mechanical work, it sure can. But it won't go on the important side quests. The ones that set the direction for the recommendation. There's no one-size-fits-all playbook in SEO. Every site and every challenge weighs a different mix of factors. So the best recommendations never come from one screen. Halfway through, a sub-question shows up that charts the course. To answer it, the best SEOs leave the task entirely. We log into another tool. We fire up an MCP we never planned on using. We crawl a competitor's site to settle one detail. That's the side quest. Nobody sees it. It's usually what makes the recommendation work. AI doesn't have the experience to know the detour is even needed. Not because it can't run the tool. Because it doesn't know: that the question exists, which tool answers it, how to get the answer. It'll crawl a competitor. But only after we tell it to. Knowing to send it on that side quest IS the job. If you read this nodding, you're not the one getting replaced. You're the one with 52 tabs open, knee-deep in a side quest..
